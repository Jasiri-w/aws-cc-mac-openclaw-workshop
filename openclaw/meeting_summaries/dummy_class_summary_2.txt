
# **Course Summary: Media Literacy & Digital Culture (MDIA 1020)**

## **Course Overview**
MDIA 1020 explores how digital platforms shape communication, identity, and public discourse. Students learn to critically evaluate online media, analyze algorithmic influence, and create responsible digital content. The course blends theory, discussion, and applied creative work.

## **Coursework & Deadlines**

### **1. Weekly Reading Reflections**
- **Description:** Short written responses (300–400 words) analyzing assigned readings.  
- **Weight:** 15%  
- **Deadline:** Every Monday at 10:00 AM  

---

### **2. Group Presentation**
- **Description:** Teams present a case study on a digital platform (e.g., TikTok, Reddit, Instagram) and analyze its cultural impact.  
- **Weight:** 20%  
- **Presentation Week:** **October 6–10**  
- **Slides Due:** **October 5 at 11:59 PM**

---

### **3. Research Essay**
- **Description:** A 2,500‑word essay examining a topic such as misinformation, online identity, or digital activism.  
- **Weight:** 30%  
- **Proposal Due:** **October 24**  
- **Annotated Bibliography:** **November 7**  
- **Final Essay Due:** **November 28 at 11:59 PM**

---

### **4. Digital Media Project**
- **Description:** Students create a short multimedia piece (video, podcast, or interactive webpage) demonstrating a concept from the course.  
- **Weight:** 35%  
- **Draft Check‑In:** **December 3**  
- **Final Submission:** **December 12 at 11:59 PM**  
- **In‑Class Showcase:** **December 15**
